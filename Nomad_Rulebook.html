Chapter 01

Basics

Setting

Set in the 27th century, Nomad is a science fiction LARP that explores concepts such as war, imperialism, and corporatism. Multiple factions exist with a vested interest in Cressida, a newly colonized world in which Nomad takes place. Several of these factions are in covert—and sometimes overt—conflict with each other, using Cressida as a staging ground to settle their disputes.

Player Characters

Every player portrays the role of a Player Character (PC). These characters are new settlers or residents of Cressida and are representatives of a faction. When a player creates their character, they choose a faction for that character to be a part of, and a class that determines their skills and abilities.

What to Expect

Nomad is a simulated combat LARP usually played in either a single day or weekend format and is a Full Immersion experience, where Player Characters and Non-Player Characters are expected to have setting-appropriate costuming and behavior.

Over the course of an event, a player will arrive on site and check in with the Logistics Team, where they receive their character sheet and are assigned an NPC shift. After Sign-In, a player should claim sleeping space (if applicable), change into character costuming and gather any appropriate Physreps (Physical Representation—an item that represents an in-game object). At a designated time, Opening Announcements will be held, followed by Game On, when all players are considered to be "In-Character." At the end of the event, Game Off will be called, followed by closing announcements and site cleanup.

Safety

Safety is of chief importance at Nomad. Nomad is a "lightest touch" combat game, meaning that combat strikes are expected to be made with the lightest force needed to be felt, and may only be made with approved foam weaponry. Protective, shatterproof eyewear is required to participate.

Safety Calls

HOLD — A pause in action. Stand still, repeat "Hold!" loudly, and await instructions. May be called by anyone for any reason, including unsafe conditions.

MEDIC — Signals a need for real-life medical attention. Treat like a Hold. Only use for suspected actual injury.

Non-Combat Designation

A player may be designated Non-Combat, represented by wearing a bright orange headband. These players may not be engaged in physical combat roleplay. An attacking character may gesture at them once per second and declare an attack or effect instead. They may respond using defensive skills as normal.

Social safety is paramount at Nomad events. Any discrimination based on Race, Gender Identity, Sexuality, Religion or Disability will not be tolerated. Physical touch without express consent is prohibited. Out-of-play aggression or intimidation will not be tolerated. Anonymous and non-anonymous reporting is available.

NPC Shift

Each event, players perform a Non-Player Character shift, portraying characters necessary for plot and story. Shifts are typically 2 hours for single-day events or 4 hours for multi-day events. Player preferences regarding type of roleplay (combat, social, etc.) will be kept in consideration, and any disability needs will be reasonably accommodated. A limited number of Opt-Out tickets are available.

Health Points

A measure of how hardy a character is. When reduced to zero, a character enters Bleedout for five minutes. During bleedout, a character cannot stand or move faster than a slow crawl. When Stable, a character is at zero HP and cannot attack—any damage sends them back into Bleedout. Every re-entry to Bleedout from Stable reduces the timer by one minute (minimum one minute).

Skill Points

Used to activate skills and abilities. Resets every quarter (0600, 1200, 1800, 0000).

Resource Points

Used in the production of items. Refreshes every day at midnight.

Shock

Measures the number of times a character can be reconstructed via backup clones. When a Bleedout timer expires, Shock drops by 1. The character enters a dead state for 5–15 minutes, then respawns with 1 HP at a designated respawn area after an additional 10 minutes.

Armor & Shield Points

Armor Points are temporary HP from wearing armor, depleted before standard HP. They do not regenerate until the armor is repaired.

Shield Points are temporary HP from energy shields, always depleted first. To recharge, take a knee, state "Recharging Shields" and remain immobile for 15 seconds. Every shield has a corresponding LED color that must be worn.

Attacks

When making an attack, declare the damage amount and any taglines unless the damage is 1 with no tagline. Defenders have 3 seconds to respond with a defensive effect. Attacks cannot target the head, hands, feet, or groin. After 3 consecutive melee attacks, the attacker must pause for 1 second. Characters may carry 50 rounds of ammunition at a time unless modified by skills or items.

Chapter 02

Character Creation

Concepts

Every character should begin with an idea: Down On Their Luck Space Captain, Anti-Corporate Saboteur, or Skilled Executive Protector. Think about backstory elements as you read through the rules, considering what sort of story they will enable you to tell.

Steps

1. Choose a Faction and Sub-faction — Determines base stats, training benefits, social group, and story role.

2. Choose a Class and Path — Templates that specialize your skills and abilities.

3. Spend Starting 18 XP — Purchase skills and stats according to the XP cost table.

4. Choose a Loadout — Starting equipment.

Militant Factions

Some sub-factions are militant, acting as an opt-in for combat PvP. By choosing a militant faction, a player agrees that other characters may engage them in combat. Marshals are still required for PvP, and any character death requires the initiating character to check-in with the other party.

Factions

Every character belongs to a Faction. When a Subfaction is chosen, Base Stats (5/5/5/5) are modified by the subfaction's stat modifiers. Subfactions grant Training Benefits (learned automatically), and at games 3, 6, 9, 12, 18, and 24, a character gains a Faction or Subfaction benefit—or may instead gain a rank.

They are the first "official" colonists on Cressida, backed by the full weight of the United Coalition of Planets.

Rigorous training and aptitude allow them to stand out. Their intense cohesion leads to a socialistic internal structure, pooling resources among themselves. Tolerance: Moderate.

Chapter 05

Combat Rules

Ranged Combat

Performed using foam-firing toys ("blasters"). Max 100 FPS for HIR-style, 85 FPS for dart-style. Use Nerf, Adventure Force, or X-Shot brand ammo only. Types: Primary (two-handed), Secondary (one-handed), Bow (single-action), Thrown (foam, ≤12"), Heavy (two-handed, requires training). Action types: Single Action, Semi-Automatic, Fully Automatic (always calls 1 damage).

Melee Combat

Foam weapons with a non-wood, non-metal core wrapped in soft foam. Swing in a 45–90° arc with lightest touch. No hooking, trapping, or thrusting. Flurry cap: 3 consecutive attacks, then 1-second pause. Types: Primary (26–46"), Secondary (12–26"), Fisticuff (12–26", counts as both primary and secondary), Two-Hand (46–72").

Troubleshooting Combat

Hacking via neural implants using packets (6" fabric squares filled with birdseed). State the ability call, throw the packet. Hits on character, weapons, or worn items apply the effect.

Armor

Standard Armor — Relaxed physrep, lower armor value. Power Armor — Extensive physreps, higher value and effects. Physical Shields — Carried on one arm, max 5' × 2.5'. Block melee and thrown; ranged and troubleshooting attacks that hit shields are considered hits. Types: Light (woven fibers), Medium (cloth + plating or leather), Heavy (leather + metal/plastic plates), Power (heavy + tech elements, lights, articulating frame).

Activated attacks (uses SP/RP/items, <1 min effects): Max 20 damage (unless a set number).

Chapter 06

Frontier Rules

The General Store

A location staffed by the Logistics Team for managing item creation skills. Kiosks allow characters to register item creation in advance for efficient pickup.

Material Gathering

Materials fall into three categories: Organic (cooking, hermetics), Inorganic, and Manufactured (buildings, gadgets). Gathered via Homestead Items (3 max per game, spend RP), Skills, and Expeditions (led by Ridgerunners).

Item Creation

Digipressing — Rapid fabrication using Digipress Chips. Spend RP, wait 5 minutes, items last until next RP refresh.

Crafting — Obtain a Schematic, gather materials, use a Workspace. Items are permanent but require more steps and time.

Modifications & Accessories

Mods affect weapons and power armor (one per type slot). Accessories are wearable items granting effects (one per slot).

Augments

Cybernetic Augments (replacement limbs, neural computers) reduce Skill Points by augment tier on install. Genetic Augments (pheromone glands, enhanced eyes) reduce Health Points by tier. Only installable by characters with Surgery skills.

Disease

Contracted during play. A marshall marks your sheet, and you receive a Disease Card from the General Store. Players can gather Sample cards via Study skills, convert them to Clue cards, and ultimately create vaccines, cures, or weaponized strains.

Teaching & Learning

Any character can teach a skill: 5 minutes of teaching RP per tier. Stats can be self-learned with 10 minutes of contemplation RP.

Convoy Jobs & Vehicles

Convoy Jobs represent trade, cargo transport, or banditry—using RP and vehicles. Each job has Cargo Value, Cost, Danger Value, and Reward. Vehicles have Fuel Cost, Capacity, Offense, Defense, and Hardiness. Warning: convoy jobs carry real risk, including possible character death.

Missions: Patrols, Forays, Expeditions

Patrols — Combat missions led by Officers. Forays — Exploration missions led by Astral Cartographers. Expeditions — Material gathering missions led by Ridgerunners. All require a Marshall among participants.

Chapter 07

Skullduggery

Stealth

Raise the Stealth Sign (hand on opposite shoulder) or wear a purple headband. While stealthed, you cannot be perceived without specialized skills. Broken by the Notice effect. Some skills allow attacking from stealth with melee or ranged weapons with the Quiet modifier.

Locks

Physical Locks — Picked with skills/items, time based on tier. Toyetic elements may reduce time.

Electronic Locks — Hacked with skills/items, time based on tier. Toyetic elements may reduce time.

Hack Fragments

Specialized software (Offensive or Defensive) used at Terminals. Offensive: Find Hacking Marshal → specify target → spend resources → defense check → Hack Time → if uninterrupted, effects are delivered. Defensive: Simpler—find Marshal, spend resources, effects applied after Hack Time. Defensive fragments can target willing characters.

Heists

Covert missions for sabotage, theft, or corporate espionage. Led by Spies, Silvertongues, or characters with the Ringleader Aptitude. Require a Marshall among participants.

Surveillance, Rumors & Intel

Surveillance Gear — Hidden with SP investment. Finding it requires spending more SP than were used to place it. Must be in place for 4 hours.

Rumors — Random character info received at check-in. Not always true.

Intel List — Secrets a character has accumulated. Up to 3 may be marked as Secrets (protected from random access). MANTIS affiliation is never revealed via Intel.

Disguise

A disguised character is treated as a separate entity for meta knowledge purposes. Disguise ends when affected by Bound By Law.

Chapter 08

Out of Play

Community Standards

All participants must treat others with dignity and respect. Nomad reserves the right to refuse service to protect its community. Players must be of legal age. Out-of-play bigotry, discrimination, sexual harassment, non-consensual physical touch, mood-affecting substances, and concealed prior disciplinary actions from other LARPs are all prohibited. Sexual intercourse is prohibited at Nomad due to communal sleeping spaces.

Battlefield Check System

Make a fist against your chest while making eye contact. Response: thumbs-up (willing to continue) or thumbs-down (unwilling). Anything other than a clear thumbs-up is a thumbs-down. If a player responds negatively, ensure their wellbeing and offer to exit the scene.

Character vs. Character Conflict (CvC)

Combat CvC

Requires a Marshall (of a different faction). Battlefield Check required if potential for death or permanent harm. If the other player reacts negatively, CvC stops immediately. Pre-negotiate whenever possible. 5+ character CvC requires a staff marshal or New Player Advocate.

Economic CvC

Covers theft and duplicitous economic behavior. Theft requires a Marshal. Consider potential for negative emotional response and pre-negotiate when appropriate.

Social CvC

Covers negative social skill use and malicious social actions. Requires a Marshal for negative social effects. Consider emotional impact and pre-negotiate.

CvC Opt-Out

Register at Operations and don a white armband. Players should avoid Combat CvC with opted-out players and must pre-negotiate any CvC. Opted-out players may not initiate non-negotiated Combat CvC.
